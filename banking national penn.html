Banking with National Penn

National Penn is a large bank located in Boyerstown, Pennsylvania. The bank currently holds assets of $1.8 billion, making it a sizable institution. Those interested in a certificate of deposit will find that they are insured by FDIC for up to $250,000. This is great for ensuring a bit more security, especially if you want to save for your future or even retirement. With a number of CDs on offer, you should take a look at the rates National Penn offers to find the best ones.

Certificates of Deposit

Fixed Rate CDs are on offer for customers and you can choose between three months to five years. There are also other options available, such as a Ready Access CD. With Ready Access, you can keep your money in for seven days before withdrawing it without incurring a penalty. Those who use National Penn’s Certificates of Deposit must make sure they have a minimum deposit of $500. IRA in this case is a separate concern.

With a sixty-month CD, you can avail of a 1.10 per cent APY. Thirty-month CDs offer customers 0.60 per cent APY. For a year, an APY of 0.30 applies. Those who choose a five-month deal can expect a 0.15 per cent APY.

Opening a Checking Account with National Penn

If you’re looking to open a checking account then National Penn has some interesting choices detailed on their website. According to their site, the bank states that they recognize the difference between people in terms of needs and requirements. They therefore offer a range of different options depending on individual customer needs.

Many of National Penn’s checking accounts also come with a number of free features. These include free online banking and direct debit. Checking accounts aren’t the only type of account on offer: you can also choose from a number of standard savings accounts. Traditional savings accounts ensure a safe place to save money but you can also get 24-hour access and an ATM card along with it. According to National Penn’s website, customers must have a daily balance of at least §250.00.

Choosing the right CD rate will require some thought. If in doubt, you can find more information on the bank’s website. All information presented in this article is true as of 10 December 2013.
